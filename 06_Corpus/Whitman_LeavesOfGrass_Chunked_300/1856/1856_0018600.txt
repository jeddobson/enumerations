Is it you, then, that thought yourself less? Is it 
you that thought the President greater than you? or the 
rich better off than you? or the educated wiser than 
you?Because you are greasy or pimpled, or that you was 
once drunk, or a thief, or diseased, or rheumatic, or 
a prostitute, or are so now, or from frivolity or 
impotence, or that you are no scholar, and never saw 
your name in print, do you give in that you 
are any less immortal?Souls of men and women! it is 
not you I call unseen, unheard, untouchable and untouch- ing,It 
is not you I go argue pro and con about, 
and to settle whether you are alive or no,I own 
publicly who you are, if nobody else owns —I see 
and hear you, and what you give and take,What is 
there you cannot give and take? I see not merely 
that you are polite or white-faced, married, single, citizens of 
old states, citizens of new states, eminent in some profession, 
a lady or gentleman in a parlor, or dressed in 
the jail uniform, or pulpit uniform,Not only the free Utahan, 
Kansian, Arkansian — not only the free Cuban, not merely
