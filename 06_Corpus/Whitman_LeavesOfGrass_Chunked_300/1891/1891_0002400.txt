18 STARTING FROM PAUMANOK.1STARTING from fish-shape Paumanok where I was 
born, Well-begotten, and rais'd by a perfect mother, After roaming 
many lands, lover of populous pavements, Dweller in Mannahatta my 
city, or on southern savannas, Or a soldier camp'd or 
carrying my knapsack and gun, or a miner in California, 
Or rude in my home in Dakota's woods, my diet 
meat, my drink from the spring, Or withdrawn to muse 
and meditate in some deep recess, Far from the clank 
of crowds intervals passing rapt and happy, Aware of the 
fresh free giver the flowing Missouri, aware of mighty Niagara, 
Aware of the buffalo herds grazing the plains, the hirsute 
and strong-breasted bull, Of earth, rocks, Fifth-month flowers experienced, stars, 
rain, snow, my amaze, Having studied the mocking-bird's tones and 
the flight of the mountain-hawk, And heard at dawn the 
unrivall'd one, the hermit thrush from the swamp-cedars, Solitary, singing 
in the West, I strike up for a New World.
