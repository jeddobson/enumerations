let it go yet, funeral though it is, Let it 
remain back there on its nail suspended, With pink, blue, 
yellow, all blanch'd, and the white now gray and ashy, 
One wither'd rose put years ago for thee, dear friend; 
But I do not forget thee. Hast thou then faded? 
Is the odor exhaled? Are the colors, vitalities, dead? No, 
while memories subtly play—the past vivid as ever; For but 
last night I woke, and in that spectral ring saw 
thee, Thy smile, eyes, face, calm, silent, loving as ever: 
So let the wreath hang still awhile within my eye-reach, 
It is not yet dead to me, nor even pallid.
