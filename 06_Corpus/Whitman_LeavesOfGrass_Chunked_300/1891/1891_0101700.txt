surrender'd to our troops, imprison'd and literally died of "a 
broken heart," at Fort Moultrie. He sicken'd of his confinement—the 
doctor and officers made every allowance and kindness possible for 
him; then the close:]WHEN his hour for death had come, 
He slowly rais'd himself from the bed on the floor, 
Drew on his war-dress, shirt, leggings, and girdled the belt 
around his waist, Call'd for vermilion paint (his looking-glass was 
held before him,) Painted half his face and neck, his 
wrists, and back-hands. Put the scalp-knife carefully in his belt—then 
lying down, resting a moment, Rose again, half sitting, smiled,
