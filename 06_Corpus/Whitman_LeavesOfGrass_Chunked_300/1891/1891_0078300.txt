defeat at Brooklyn, Washington stands inside the lines, he stands 
on the intrench'd hills amid a crowd of officers, His 
face is cold and damp, he cannot repress the weeping 
drops, He lifts the glass perpetually to his eyes, the 
color is blanch'd from his cheeks, He sees the slaughter 
of the southern braves confided to him by their parents. 
The same at last and at last when peace is 
declared, He stands in the room of the old tavern, 
the well-belov'd soldiers all pass through, The officers speechless and 
slow draw near in their turns, The chief encircles their 
necks with his arm and kisses them on the cheek, 
He kisses lightly the wet cheeks one after another, he 
shakes hands and bids good-by to the army. 6Now what
