The red squaw staid all the forenoon, and toward the 
middle of the afternoon she went away, O my mother 
was loth to have her go away, All the week 
she thought of her, she watch'd for her many a 
month, She remember'd her many a winter and many a 
summer, But the red squaw never came nor was heard 
of there again. 7A show of the summer softness—a contact
