DEATH.AS at thy portals also death, Entering thy sovereign, dim, 
illimitable grounds, To memories of my mother, to the divine 
blending, maternity, To her, buried and gone, yet buried not, 
gone not from me, (I see again the calm benignant 
face fresh and beautiful still, I sit by the form 
in the coffin, I kiss and kiss convulsively again the 
sweet old lips, the cheeks, the closed eyes in the 
coffin;) To her, the ideal woman, practical, spiritual, of all 
of earth, life, love, to me the best, I grave 
a monumental line, before I go, amid these songs, And 
set a tombstone here. MY LEGACY.THE business man the acquirer 
vast, After assiduous years surveying results, preparing for departure, Devises 
houses and lands to his children, bequeaths stocks, goods, funds 
for a school or hospital, Leaves money to certain companions 
to buy tokens, souvenirs of gems and gold. But I, 
my life surveying, closing, With nothing to show to devise 
from its idle years, Nor houses nor lands, nor tokens 
of gems or gold for my friends, Yet certain remembrances 
of the war for you, and after you, And little 
souvenirs of camps and soldiers, with my love, I bind 
together and bequeath in this bundle of songs. PENSIVE ON
