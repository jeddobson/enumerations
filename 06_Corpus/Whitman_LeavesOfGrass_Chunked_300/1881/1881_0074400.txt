half-envelop'd her face, Her step was free and elastic, and 
her voice sounded exquisitely as she spoke. My mother look'd 
in delight and amazement at the stranger,She look'd at the 
freshness of her tall-borne face and full and pliant limbs, 
The more she look'd upon her she loved her,Never before 
had she seen such wonderful beauty and purity,She made her 
sit on a bench by the jamb of the fireplace, 
she cook'd food for her, She had no work to 
give her, but she gave her remembrance and fondness. The 
red squaw staid all the forenoon, and toward the middle 
of the afternoon she went away, O my mother was 
loth to have her go away,All the week she thought 
of her, she watch'd for her many a month, She 
remember'd her many a winter and many a summer,But the 
red squaw never came nor was heard of there again.7A
