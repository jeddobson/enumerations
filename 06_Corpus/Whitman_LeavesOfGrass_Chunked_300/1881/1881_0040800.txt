O swift again far out in the ocean the wounded 
whale, settling, running to windward, tows me, Again I see 
him rise to breathe, we row close again,I see a 
lance driven through his side, press'd deep, turn'd in the 
wound, Again we back off, I see him settle again, 
the life is leaving him fast, As he rises he 
spouts blood, I see him swim in circles narrower and 
narrower, swiftly cutting the water—I see him die, He gives 
one convulsive leap in the centre of the circle, and 
then falls flat and still in the bloody foam. O 
the old manhood of me, my noblest joy of all!My 
children and grand-children, my white hair and beard,My largeness, calmness, 
majesty, out of the long stretch of my life.O ripen'd 
joy of womanhood! O happiness at last!I am more than 
eighty years of age, I am the most venerable mother, 
How clear is my mind—how all people draw nigh to 
me!What attractions are these beyond any before? what bloom more
