passed all over their bodies. An unseen hand also passed 
over their bodies, It descended tremblingly from their temples and 
ribs.The young men float on their backs, their white bellies 
swell to the sun . . . . they do 
not ask who seizes fast to them, They do not 
know who puffs and declines with pendant and bending arch, 
They do not think whom they souse with spray. The 
butcher-boy puts off his killing-clothes, or sharpens his knife at 
the stall in the market, I loiter enjoying his repartee 
and his shuffle and breakdown. Blacksmiths with grimed and hairy 
chests environ the anvil, Each has his main-sledge . . 
. . they are all out . . . . 
there is a great heat in the fire. From the 
cinder-strewed threshold I follow their movements, The lithe sheer of
