in my face while I snuff the sidle of evening, 
Talk honestly, for no one else hears you, and I 
stay only a minute longer. Do I contradict myself? Very 
well then . . . . I contradict myself; I 
am large . . . . I contain multitudes. I 
concentrate toward them that are nigh . . . . 
I wait on the door-slab. Who has done his day's 
work and will soonest be through with his supper? Who 
wishes to walk with me? Will you speak before I 
am gone? Will you prove already too late? The spotted 
hawk swoops by and accuses me . . . . 
he complains of my gab and my loitering. I too 
am not a bit tamed . . . . I
