. . . . it is not in this book, 
It is for you whoever you are . . . 
. it is no farther from you than your hearing 
and sight are from you, It is hinted by nearest 
and commonest and readiest . . . . it is 
not them, though it is endlessly provoked by them . 
. . . What is there ready and near you 
now? You may read in many languages and read nothing 
about it;You may read the President's message and read nothing 
about it there, Nothing in the reports from the state 
department or treasury department . . . . or in 
the daily papers, or the weekly papers, Or in the 
census returns or assessors' returns or prices current or any 
accounts of stock. The sun and stars that float in 
the open air . . . . the appleshaped earth 
and we upon it . . . . surely the 
drift of them is something grand; I do not know 
what it is except that it is grand, and that 
it is happiness, And that the enclosing purport of us 
here is not a speculation, or bon-mot or reconnoissance, And 
that it is not something which by luck may turn
