hence, or ever so many hundred years hence, others will 
see them, Will enjoy the sunset, the pouring in of 
the flood- tide, the falling back to the sea of 
the ebb-tide. 5 It avails not, neither time or place—distance 
avails not, I am with you, you men and women 
of a generation, or ever so many generations hence, I 
project myself—also I return—I am with you, and know how 
it is. 6 Just as you feel when you look 
on the river and sky, so I felt, Just as 
any of you is one of a living crowd, I 
was one of a crowd, Just as you are refreshed 
by the gladness of the river, and the bright flow, 
I was refreshed, Just as you stand and lean on 
the rail, yet hurry with the swift current, I stood, 
yet was hurried, Just as you look on the numberless 
masts of ships, and the thick-stemmed pipes of steamboats, I 
looked. 7 I too many and many a time crossed 
the river, the sun half an hour high, I watched
