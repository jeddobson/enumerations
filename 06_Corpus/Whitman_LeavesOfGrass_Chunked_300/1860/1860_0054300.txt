lips, sad and tremulous. 10.INQUIRING, tireless, seeking that yet unfound,I, 
a child, very old, over waves, toward the house of 
maternity, the land of migrations, look afar, Look off the 
shores of my Western Sea—having arrived at last where I 
am—the circle almost circled; For coming westward from Hindustan, from 
the vales of Kashmere, From Asia—from the north—from the God, 
the sage, and the hero, From the south—from the flowery 
peninsulas, and the spice islands, Now I face the old 
home again—looking over to it, joyous, as after long travel, 
growth, and sleep; But where is what I started for,
