8 I have perceived that to be with those I 
like is enough, To stop in company with the rest 
at evening is enough, To be surrounded by beautiful, curious, 
breathing, laughing flesh is enough, To pass among them, or 
touch any one, or rest my arm ever so lightly 
round his or her neck for a moment—what is this, 
then? I do not ask any more delight—I swim in 
it, as in a sea. 9 There is something in 
staying close to men and women, and looking on them, 
and in the contact and odor of them, that pleases 
the Soul well, All things please the Soul—but these please 
the Soul well. 10 This is the female form,A divine 
nimbus exhales from it from head to foot,It attracts with 
fierce undeniable attraction,I am drawn by its breath as if 
I were no more than a helpless vapor—all falls aside
