not know how longingly I look upon you,You must be 
he I was seeking, or she I was seeking, (it 
comes to me, as of a dream,)I have somewhere surely 
lived a life of joy with you,All is recall'd as 
we flit by each other, fluid, affection- ate, chaste, matured,You 
grew up with me, were a boy with me, or 
a girl with me,I ate with you, and slept with 
you—your body has become not yours only, nor left my 
body mine only,You give me the pleasure of your eyes, 
face, flesh, as we pass—you take of my beard, breast, 
hands, in return,I am not to speak to you—I am 
to think of you when I sit alone, or wake 
at night alone,I am to wait—I do not doubt I 
am to meet you again,I am to see to it 
that I do not lose you. This Moment, Yearning and
